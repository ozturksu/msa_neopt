## Research Question

> To what extent does replacing the single-scale Transformer backbone in NeOpt with a Multi-Scale Attention CNN improve the economic performance of battery storage dispatch for Demand Charge Reduction on EirGrid historical data?

---

## Background

Large electricity consumers in Ireland face demand charges under the Irish Single Electricity Market (SEM) where a single 15-minute peak can account for up to **70% of a monthly electricity bill**. Battery storage can eliminate this peak — but only if dispatched at exactly the right moment.

The standard approach (Predict-then-Optimise, PTO) trains a model to minimise forecast error, then passes that forecast to a separate optimiser. The problem: a forecast that is accurate on average can still be wrong at the critical 15-minute slot that determines the monthly peak.

**Decision-Focused Learning (DFL)** fixes this by training the model directly on the cost of the dispatch decision, not on forecast error.

## Results

| Model | Loss | Val Regret | Test Regret | Peak Reduction | Parameters |
|-------|------|-----------|------------|---------------|-----------|
| PTO (MultiDeT) | MSE | 0.005 MSE | 1.84% | 5.0% | 4.7M |
| SSPO Ablation | Surrogate | 0.051 | 0.84% | 9.2% | 2.5M |
| **MSA-NeOpt (Ours)** | **SPO+** | **0.0082** | **0.19%** | **20.9%** | **0.6M** |
| NeOpt (SPO+) | SPO+ | 0.0105 | 0.04% | 26.2% | 2.5M |

**Key finding:** MSA-NeOpt achieves 9.6× lower regret than the MSE baseline with 74% fewer parameters than the single-scale Transformer.

conda create -n msa_neopt_env python=3.10
conda activate msa_neopt_env

## Key Equations

**SPO+ forward loss:**

$$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{B}\sum_{i=1}^{B} \frac{(\hat{p}_i - p_i^*)^+}{p_i^*}$$

**SPO+ backward gradient:**

$$\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \hat{y}_{i,t}} = \frac{s(2\hat{y}_i - y_i) - s^*(y_i)}{H}$$

**Relative regret (primary metric):**

$$\text{Regret}_i = \frac{(\hat{p}_i - p_i^*)^+}{p_i^*} \times 100\%$$

where $\hat{p}_i$ = greedy dispatch peak from model prediction, $p_i^*$ = oracle peak from perfect foresight, $H = 96$ slots.
